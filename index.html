🎂 The birthday plan

Kai turns 40 on the water

Birthday plan Thursday July 30 · 9:30 morning slot 3 hours · GoBoat's self-drive max Seats 8 · three generations, one boat Morning start · dock opens 9:30

      Booking

      • Where: goboat.dk ↗, Islands Brygge location. July is peak season, so book ahead; morning slots go first.
      • Length: options are 1, 2, or 3 hours; 3 is the max and the birthday pick.
      • Price: roughly €100 for 2 hours as a reference; confirm the 3-hour price at checkout.
      • Picnic basket add-on: order 24h+ ahead, or bring our own spread.
      • Cancellation: full refund up to 72h before; changes or a credit note up to 24h.

      Why a morning slot

      • Calmer water, fewer tour boats, easier steering for the birthday captain.
      • Nyhavn's mouth clogs with canal tours by midday; early boats glide through.
      • No toilet on board, so time the hop-offs; every marked mooring has toilets close by.
      • Home by early afternoon with both kids still standing (naps intact).

      Plan ahead & logistics

      Are restaurant reservations refundable?

      Short answer: usually yes, with no money down. Most Copenhagen restaurants book through DinnerBooking or OpenTable, which take no card and charge nothing if you cancel in time (typically up to a few hours before). Just cancel through the confirmation email or app so you do not become a no-show.

      • Casual and mid-range: free to cancel, no card required. Courtesy cutoff is usually same-day, a few hours ahead.
      • Tasting-menu and high-end: often prepaid and non-refundable. Noma requires cancellation at least 14 days ahead or the prepayment is lost.
      • Rule of thumb: read the policy line at booking. If it asks for a card, treat it as non-refundable inside the stated window.

      Getting around & finding more

      • Metro: fast, driverless, runs all night. Easiest with two little ones plus a stroller.
      • Cargo bike: the hosts' Babboe (seats up to 4 kids) lives in the base 1 yard through Jul 26; heaviest kid closest to the driver, always both locks. From base 2, Donkey Republic e-bikes and the metro take over.
      • Playground finder: legeplads.kk.dk maps every city playground.
      • Kids' events: bornibyen.dk/koebenhavn (use Google Translate) lists what's on near your dates.

      • Tivoli: buy ride wristbands online; check the Friday “Fredagsrock” concert if you want one.
      • CopenHill & Sauna Club Sydhavn: book climbing, skiing, or a sauna session ahead.

      No booking needed: metro and regional trains, Nationalmuseet, DAC, Experimentarium, Designmuseum, playgrounds, Reffen, harbour bath, Bakken, and Dyrehaven. Frilandsmuseet is free but check open days. Confirm the Natural History Museum isn't mid-renovation.

      Tourist passes

      Pick: skip an all-trip pass. Buy two 72-hour Copenhagen Card DISCOVER cards (one per adult) for a single concentrated sightseeing block. Each adult brings two kids aged 3–11 free, and ages 0–2 are free, so two adult cards cover all four kids+parents.

      • Covers: all-zone transit (including the train to Louisiana in Humlebæk, Klampenborg for Dyrehaven, and the airport) plus Tivoli, the Zoo, Louisiana, Rosenborg, and 80+ sites.
      • Why not 3 weeks: the clock runs continuously, and most days are free playgrounds, Reffen, swims, and the free Nationalmuseet.
      • Gotchas: Tivoli rides cost extra (wristbands); GoBoat, CopenHill, and the Zoo panda restaurant aren't card attractions. Add the kids' cards at purchase; the clock starts at first scan.
      • Off-card days: kids under 12 ride free with a paying adult, so use the DOT Tickets app or a Rejsekort.
